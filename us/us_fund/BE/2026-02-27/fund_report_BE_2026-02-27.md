## Chapter 2: Company Overview & Business Model

### 2.1 Company Profile

Bloom Energy Corporation was founded in 2001 and listed on NYSE in 2018.

**Employees**: ~2,214

**Location**: San Jose, California, USA

### 2.2 Business Model Evolution

Bloom Energy began as a pure-play solid oxide fuel cell (SOFC) manufacturer, providing on-site power generation primarily to commercial and industrial (C&I) customers. The company has evolved through three key phases:

**Phase 1: Technology Development (2001-2018)**  
Focus on R&D and pilot deployments, establishing SOFC technology viability and securing early commercial customers.

**Phase 2: Commercialization & Growth (2018-2023)**  
Public listing and scale-up; expanded customer base across telecom, manufacturing, healthcare, education, and retail sectors. Introduced service business model with long-term service contracts attached to every product sale.

**Phase 3: AI & Hyperscale Pivot (2024-Present)**  
Strategic pivot to serve data center and AI infrastructure market, launching 800V DC native platform and absorption chiller solutions. Significant expansion in hyperscale and neo-cloud customer base from 1 to 6+ customers.

### 2.3 Core Competitive Advantages

1. **Solid Oxide Fuel Cell Technology**: Proprietary SOFC platform converts natural gas, biogas, hydrogen, or blends into electricity through non-combustion electrochemical process, achieving higher efficiency (37% product margin) than many alternatives.

2. **Asset-Light Manufacturing**: Unique manufacturing approach enables capacity expansion with ROI measured in months versus years for legacy competitors, providing scalability flexibility.

3. **Service Revenue Moat**: 100% service attach rate creates long-term recurring revenue stream (~$14B service backlog), supporting customer relationships and providing visibility.

4. **Time-to-Power Leadership**: Demonstrated ability to deploy hyperscale AI factory orders in 55 days against 90-day commitments, faster than customers can build their own greenfield facilities.

5. **Fuel Flexibility**: Systems can operate on multiple fuel types (natural gas, biogas, hydrogen), providing adaptability as energy transition progresses.
