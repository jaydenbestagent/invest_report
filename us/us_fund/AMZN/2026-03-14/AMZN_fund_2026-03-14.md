## Chapter 2: Company Overview & Business Model

> **Key Takeaway**: Amazon has evolved from an online bookstore into the world's most diversified technology conglomerate, with dominant positions across e-commerce, cloud computing, digital advertising, and subscription services.

### 2.1 Company Profile

Amazon.com, Inc. was founded in 1994 by Jeff Bezos and listed on NASDAQ in 1997.

**Employees**: ~1,576,000

Amazon operates through three reportable segments: North America (e-commerce), International (e-commerce), and Amazon Web Services (AWS, cloud computing). The company also generates significant revenue from advertising, subscription services (Prime), and physical stores (Whole Foods).

### 2.2 Business Model Evolution

Amazon's business model has undergone a remarkable transformation over the past decade. Starting as an online retailer, the company built out its cloud computing division (AWS) which now generates the majority of operating income. More recently, Amazon has become a top-3 digital advertising platform by leveraging its massive first-party shopper data. The company's flywheel model — where each business unit reinforces the others (Prime drives e-commerce, e-commerce drives advertising, AWS provides infrastructure for all) — creates a self-reinforcing competitive advantage that is difficult to replicate.

### 2.3 Core Competitive Advantages

1. **Logistics & Fulfillment Scale**: Largest e-commerce logistics network globally with 8B+ same/next-day items delivered to US Prime members in FY2025 (+30% YoY), creating an insurmountable cost and speed advantage
2. **AWS Cloud Leadership**: 30-32% cloud infrastructure market share with $244B backlog, deepest service portfolio (200+ services), and proprietary AI chips (Trainium, Inferentia) reducing costs 30-40% vs. alternatives
3. **First-Party Data Moat**: 200M+ Prime subscribers worldwide generate purchase-intent data that powers a $68.6B advertising business with superior targeting and measurability
